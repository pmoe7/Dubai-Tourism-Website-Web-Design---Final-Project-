Skydiving

Beginner skydivers and experienced parachutists alike will relish skydiving here in Dubai. From the rush of free fall to the mind-blowing release of the parachute to the controlled glide and precision landing, it’s a thrill you’ll never forget.

Soar like a bird over this iconic city, and see how the islands of The World are really taking shape and how Palm Jumeirah really does look like, well, a palm tree. In skydiving terms, Dubai is one of the top ‘drop zones’ in the world. So what are you waiting for?

Step out of a plane at an altitude of almost 4,000 metres and enjoy the rush of the wind. The whole experience is a full-scale immersion of the senses. With instructors who have collectively logged well over 220,000 jumps, you’ll be in good hands — and harnesses — as you jump above this iconic city. To make sure you remember the experience forever, a cameraman documents the fun.

Golf

Dubai is something of a newcomer to the world of golf, with its first grass course opening in 1988 at the Majlis at Emirates Golf Club. Since then, some of the world's greatest golf course designers have turned Dubai into a premier destination.

Home to 11 outstanding golf courses and two of the European Tour’s most prestigious tournaments, Dubai is a golfing success story. We have trophy courses and, of course, guaranteed perfect weather from September through May. We’re home to many championship contests like the Ladies Masters, the MENA Golf Tour and the Dubai Desert Classic — a tournament that celebrated its 25th anniversary in 2014.

Even if you're not a golfer, the area's many courses are must-see destinations with spectacular gardens, water features and clubhouses. They’re a far cry from Dubai's original golfing venues, where players teed off in the sand, often stopping mid-game to wait for camels to pass by.

Dubai Autodrome

Fasten your seatbelts and experience the thrill of burning rubber at the Dubai Autodrome. This fully integrated motorsports facility includes an FIA sanctioned 5.39 kilometre Race & Drive Centre and Karting track. The arena also accommodates all types of local and world-class motorsport events, with such global luminaries as Jacques Villeneuve and Jenson Button putting their skills to the test on its challenging track.

Want to give it a go yourself? You can hit the track in an Audi TT or feel the epic power of an Audi R8 V10. The single seater is for adrenaline addicts looking to feel the power of a real race car. Super light and with incredible acceleration and sharp handling, this beast allows you to hug the circuit corners at high speeds. Then of course, there are the rally cars. Hold onto your helmet as you rocket out of the corners with 315 bhp of punch

Even youngsters can discover the thrill of driving with the First Drive Experience, designed for children aged 12-18. And groups with a competitive spirit can rally together and speed through the Kartdrome. A true test of skill, you’ll compete around 17 corners on this 1.2 kilometre track. So hop into a Honda 390cc kart and put your foot to the floor —it’s time to satisfy that need for speed.

Global Village

Each year from October to April, five million people visit the ever-popular Global Village. Some come for the thrilling funfair rides, some for the food, some for the nightly cultural entertainment, and some for the authentic shopping. Here you’ll find more than 65 countries within 37 lavish pavilions showcasing their local products.

For example, you’ll find delicious honey from Yemen, exquisite carpets from Iran and Afganistan, wooden pots from Africa, Argan oil from Morocco, engraved necklaces from Spain, and dried fruits from Thailand. In the India pavilion, the largest pavilion at Global Village with more than 300 stalls, you’ll see cotton, silk, and chiffon fabrics in every colour of the rainbow made into clothes, tablecloths, and throws, and, of course, pashmina scarves.

Each pavilion, from Egypt to Italy, Vietnam to the Oman, attempts to transport visitors to their country through smell, sound, and lavish decorations. No two pavilions are the same, setting visitors on a true global adventure.

The entertainment also traverses the world with up to four nightly concerts, and artists serving up a mix of magic, music and comedy on the World Culture Stage.

Aquaventure

Aquaventure is one of Dubai’s most thrilling waterparks. In 2013 it got even better when it opened four more thrilling slides — each wrapped around the new Tower of Poseidon pyramid — and the Middle East’s longest zip line circuit.

However, you can still dive into old favourites like the Leap of Faith, a 27.5-metre plunge that carries you through a clear tube surrounded by sharks and rays. Although children need to be taller than 1.2 metres to enjoy many of the park’s slides, little ones will delight in the Splashers’ Children’s Play Area — a maze of kid-friendly tunnels, tubes, slides and climbing frames.

From the romantic beachside setting of Villa Beach, to a fun time with the family at Latitude, to a legendary night at top nightclub 360°, there’s something for every mood at our beach restaurants in Dubai.

Get the adrenaline pumping with record breaking rides and slides, in the No. 1 water park in the Middle East and Europe. Get up close and personal with sharks and rays or kick back and relax on 700 metres of pristine private beach or in the new exclusive reserved seating areas. Come and get your thrills, spills and divine relaxation at Aquaventure Waterpark.
